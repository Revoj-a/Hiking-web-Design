HIKING GEAR: Everything you will need for going on a hike

Scanning the closet but not sure what to pick? We've got you covered from head to toe. Here's what to wear hiking in every season.

You will need to make sure you have packed everything you will need when going on a long hike. It's not only importent to make sure thay you have high-quality hiking gear, but you also don;t want your backpack to be too heavy.Therefore, it is good idea to only pack the things you really need to help save space and stop your backpack from becoming too heavy. We'll show you whichi hiking essentials every beginner needs and also how to pack your backpack for going on a hike.

A practical hiking kit list to help make sure you have everything you will need for your hike.

Hitting the trail for the first time? Time for a fit check! Fashion might not be very important on the trail, but your hiking outfit can still make the differences between a fun time and miserable one. However, that doesn't mean you need to go out and buy a whole new wardrobe for your first hike-in fact, you may already have the right outfit ready to go. Dress appropriately with these tips.

CHECK THE WEATHER

It's a no-brainer: You can't pick your hiking outfit until you know what conditions you're dressing for. Keep in mind that the weather at the trailhead oi in a nearby town won't necessarily be the same as at your hiking destination. High elevations and summits tend to be colder and windier that lower spots, and storms may be more likely. Prepare for the absolute worst weather you might encounter - it's better to be overprepared than underprepared.

FOR YOU FIRST HIKING OUTFIT, USE WHAT YOU HAVE

Hiking-specific apparel is great, but for your first hike, you likely already have what you need in your closet. Moisture - wicking activewear like what you'd wear to the gym or on a run functions on most hikes, too. Choose clothing that's comfortable, flexible, and protects you from weather, spiky or poisinous plants, and bugs.

BACKPACK

A comfortable and durable backpack is crucial for carrying all your gear. Consider factors such as size, weight, and features when selecting the perfect backpack for your needs.

HYDRATION SYSTEM

Staying hydrated is crucial, so pack a water bottle or hydration reservoir that meets your capacity, weight, and ease-of-use needs.

WHAT SHOES TO WEAR HIKING

Sore feet lead to a bad time on trail. For your first few hikes, wear any sturdy, closed-toe athletic shoes you have. Sneakers are fine for most easy to moderate trails, but you may want shoes with some extra traction and support for steep hikes.

NAVIGATION TOOLS (map, compass, or GPS)

Navigating your route is essential, so bring a map, compass, or GPSdevice and familiarize yourself with their use beforehand.

FIRST-AID KIT

Carry a first-aid kit with essentials supplies to address minor injuries and medical emergencies. Customize your kit to include items specific to your needs and the nature of your hike.

MULTI-TOOL OR KNIFE

A multi-tool or knife is handy for various tasks, such as gear repair, food preparation, or emergrncy situations.

HEADLAMP OR FLASHLIGHT

Bring them on a go for illumination during early morning, evening, or emergency situations.

UNDERWEAR FOR HIKING

For your first hike, wear whatever you feel comfortable in. Got a favorite pair of boxers for working out? Chances are you'll be happy in them on the trail, too. As you start going for longer hikes, overnights, and backpacking trips, your preferences might change. Most hikers prefer synthetic or wool underwear instead of cotton. Just make sure you wear something that's comfortable for extended durations of movement - prominent seams that might cause chafing are a no-no.

LAYERING FOR A HIKE

On the trail, it's all about having options. Conditions might change as you hike, and your body temperature will fluctuate between periods of exertion and rest. Wear light layers against your body.

Unless you're hiking somewhere that night time temperatures are consistently above 79, always wear or carry a midlayer, such as a fleece or wool hoodie. It might be scorching at your car, but you never know when you might get chilly during your hike.

In colder temperatures, an insulating layer like a puffy jacket is a good idea. Stash it in your pack while you hike, and pull it on to preserve warmth while you snack or take in the views. Dor cold hikes, bring a warm hat and gloves or mittens. It's never a bad idea to pack two pairs of gloves: a light pair for while you're moving, and a warmer pair for when you're stopped.

Embarking on outdoor adventures, whether it's hiking or camping, can be an incredibly rewarding and fulfilling experience. By investing in high-quality gear, learning essentials skills, and staying informed about safety and best practices, you can maximize your enjoyment and create unforgettable memories in the great outdoors.
